Scroll animation rules

What the page should do while scrolling.

These rules are the baseline for stacking, parallax, and reveal behavior on the marketing homepage. Keep them cinematic but readable.

Motion summary

Stack, glide, reveal, then convert.

The homepage should feel like one continuous story, with sales CTAs surfacing at the right moments instead of competing all at once.

Stacking Parallax Reveal
01
Hero enters first, then the supporting copy follows.
Use staged reveal timing so the headline, subcopy, CTA, and visual all arrive in sequence. The first impression should feel intentional, not busy.
02
Parallax belongs to product images and packaging panels.
Move the image layers subtly against the scroll direction. Keep text stable so the page still feels readable and premium.
03
Stacking should happen in major section transitions.
The next section can overlap the previous one slightly with depth, shadow, and vertical offset. This makes the landing feel cinematic without needing excessive motion.
04
Every major block needs a real CTA.
Wholesale and retail must be visually dominant, while journal and story stay accessible but secondary.
05
Reduced motion must keep the structure intact.
If motion is reduced, the page should still read like a complete brand story. Animation is an enhancer, not a dependency.
